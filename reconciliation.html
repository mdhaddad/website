Victim Reconciliation

Supporting Healing and Restoration for Those Harmed

Our victim reconciliation services provide support to those who have experienced harm, offering both direct assistance and opportunities for healing dialogue when victims choose to engage. We recognize that victims' needs are diverse and honor each person's unique journey toward healing.

Services for Victims Include:

  • Individual support and advocacy throughout the justice process
  • Information about restorative options and their rights within these processes
  • Facilitated dialogue opportunities with those who caused harm (when desired and appropriate)
  • Connection to community support services and resources
  • Support in developing and communicating their needs for repair and healing

Our Approach to Victim Services:We understand that victims are the experts on their own needs and experiences. Our role is to provide information, support, and optionsâ€”never to pressure participation in any particular process. Some victims find healing through direct dialogue, others through community support, and still others through different pathways entirely.

Supporting Families and Communities:We recognize that harm often extends beyond direct victims to families and broader community members. Our reconciliation services can include support for these secondary victims and facilitate broader community healing processes.

When Reconciliation Isn't Appropriate:We carefully assess each situation to determine when reconciliation processes are appropriate and safe. We never compromise victim safety or wellbeing, and we maintain clear boundaries around cases involving ongoing danger or where power imbalances cannot be adequately addressed.
