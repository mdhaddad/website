Mediation Circles

Bringing People Together for Healing Dialogue

Our mediation circles create safe, structured spaces where those affected by harm can come together in dialogue. These circles bring together victims, offenders, and community members in a process guided by trained facilitators who understand the delicate work of restoration.

What Mediation Circles Offer:

  • A safe environment for honest, facilitated conversation about the impact of harm
  • Opportunity for victims to be heard and have their questions answered
  • Space for offenders to understand the full impact of their actions and take meaningful responsibility
  • Community input on how to repair harm and prevent future incidents
  • Collaborative development of agreements that address everyone's needs

Who Can Participate:Participation in mediation circles is always voluntary. We work with referrals from courts, probation services, community organizations, and direct requests from those seeking resolution. All parties must be ready and willing to engage authentically in the process.

The Circle Process:Our circles are grounded in Indigenous peacemaking traditions and facilitated by trained volunteers from the community. We prepare all participants individually before bringing them together, ensuring everyone understands the process and feels ready to participate meaningfully.
