Victim-Offender Mediation

Creating safe spaces for dialogue, accountability, and healing between those who have caused harm and those who have been harmed.

Mediation session - two people talking with mediator

What is Victim-Offender Mediation?

Victim-Offender Mediation is a voluntary, confidential process that brings together people who have been harmed and those responsible for the harm in a safe, structured environment.

Our trained mediators facilitate conversations that allow both parties to share their experiences, ask questions, and work toward understanding and resolution. This process focuses on healing rather than punishment, giving victims a voice and offenders the opportunity to take meaningful responsibility for their actions.

Key Benefits

  • For Victims: Get answers to questions, express impact, and have a voice in the resolution process
  • For Offenders: Understand the real impact of actions, take genuine accountability, and make meaningful amends
  • For Communities: Strengthen relationships, prevent future harm, and build collective healing

When Mediation Can Help

Victim-Offender Mediation can be valuable in many situations where harm has occurred and both parties are open to dialogue.

Common Cases Include:

  • Property crimes (theft, vandalism, burglary)
  • Minor assaults or harassment
  • Neighborhood disputes that escalated
  • Workplace conflicts involving misconduct
  • School-related incidents
  • Some cases of white-collar crime

Mediation Works Best When:

  • Both parties voluntarily agree to participate
  • There's a genuine desire for understanding
  • Safety can be ensured for all participants
  • The offender accepts responsibility for their actions
  • The victim is emotionally ready for dialogue

From Anger to Understanding

"I came into mediation angry and hurt. I left understanding that the person who robbed my store was struggling and needed help, not just punishment. We worked out a plan where he could make amends through community service at a local food bank. It wasn't just about my loss anymore - it was about preventing future harm."

- Maria, small business owner

A Second Chance

"Facing the family I hurt was the hardest thing I've ever done, but it changed my life. Hearing how my actions affected not just the victim but their whole family made it real in a way that court never did. The mediation gave me a chance to truly apologize and commit to making changes. Two years later, I'm still keeping those commitments."

- James, program participant

Ready to Begin the Healing Process?

Taking the first step toward mediation shows courage and commitment to healing. We're here to support you through every stage of the process.

For Victims

If you've been harmed and are considering mediation, we can help you explore whether this process might be right for your situation.

For Offenders

If you've caused harm and want to take meaningful responsibility, mediation can provide a path to genuine accountability and making amends.

For Referrals

Attorneys, court staff, and community members can refer cases to our program. We'll assess suitability and work with all parties involved.
