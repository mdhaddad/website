Our Approach

Restorative justice is a transformative approach to addressing harm that focuses on repairing relationships and rebuilding trust within our communities. Rather than simply punishing wrongdoing, we create safe spaces where all parties affected by harm can come together to understand its impact and work toward healing.

Our programs are designed to empower individuals, strengthen families, and build resilient communities through dialogue, accountability, and mutual support. We believe that everyone has the capacity for growth and positive change when given the right environment and support.

Bringing People Together for Healing

Victim-Offender Mediation (VOM) is a carefully facilitated meeting between victims of crime and the individuals who harmed them. These voluntary meetings take place in a safe, neutral environment with trained mediators who guide the conversation toward understanding, accountability, and healing.

How It Works

Our mediation process begins with separate preparation meetings with both parties to ensure readiness and safety. During these sessions, participants explore their feelings, expectations, and goals for the mediation. The actual mediation meeting provides a structured opportunity for victims to ask questions, express the impact of the harm, and for offenders to take responsibility and make amends.

Benefits

Participants often report reduced anxiety, increased sense of closure, and better understanding of the incident. For victims, it can provide answers to important questions and reduce fear. For offenders, it offers a chance to truly understand the impact of their actions and take meaningful steps toward repair.

Our Commitment

Every mediation is carefully screened for appropriateness and safety. Our certified mediators undergo extensive training in trauma-informed practices, conflict resolution, and restorative justice principles. We work closely with law enforcement, courts, and community partners to ensure the best possible outcomes for all participants.

Program Details

Duration: 2-4 hours

Preparation: 2-3 meetings per party

Cost: Free of charge

Location: Neutral community spaces

Who Can Participate

Adults and youth involved in property crimes, minor assaults, harassment, and other appropriate cases where both parties are willing to engage in dialogue.

Get Started

Contact us at (555) 123-HEAL to discuss whether mediation might be right for your situation. All inquiries are confidential.

Collective Healing Through Community

Community Conferencing expands the circle of healing beyond just victims and offenders to include family members, friends, community members, and other stakeholders who have been affected by harmful behavior. This collaborative approach recognizes that crime and conflict impact entire communities, not just individuals.

The Conference Process

Conferences bring together all parties affected by an incident in a carefully structured meeting. Participants sit in a circle and share their experiences, feelings, and perspectives about what happened and how it affected them. Together, they develop a plan for repair that addresses the needs of everyone involved and helps prevent future harm.

Community Involvement

Community members play vital roles as supporters, witnesses, and resources for healing. They might include teachers, employers, neighbors, spiritual leaders, or other trusted individuals who can offer ongoing support and accountability. This network helps ensure that agreements reached in the conference are sustainable and meaningful.

Long-term Impact

By involving the broader community, conferencing creates lasting change that extends beyond the immediate participants. Communities develop stronger relationships, better understanding of underlying issues, and increased capacity to prevent and address future conflicts collaboratively.

Conference Details

Duration: 3-5 hours

Participants: 6-12 people

Preparation: Individual meetings with all participants

Follow-up: Check-ins at 30, 60, and 90 days

Appropriate Cases

Youth offenses, family conflicts, workplace disputes, neighborhood issues, and cases where community support would be beneficial for healing and accountability.

Community Partners

We work with schools, employers, faith communities, and other organizations to build restorative practices throughout our community.

Circle Processes

Ancient wisdom for modern healing

Sacred Space for Dialogue

Circle processes draw on indigenous peacemaking traditions that have been used for thousands of years to resolve conflicts and build community. In a circle, all participants sit as equals, sharing a talking piece that ensures everyone has an opportunity to speak and be heard without interruption.

Types of Circles

We facilitate various types of circles including healing circles for trauma recovery, sentencing circles that involve the community in judicial decisions, support circles for individuals facing challenges, and celebration circles to honor positive achievements and milestones.

The Circle Way

Circles begin with opening ceremonies that create sacred space and establish guidelines for respectful communication. Participants share their stories, perspectives, and feelings while others listen deeply. The talking piece moves around the circle, ensuring equal participation and preventing dominance by any single voice.

Cultural Sensitivity

We honor the indigenous origins of circle processes while adapting them respectfully for diverse communities. Our circle keepers are trained in cultural humility and work to create inclusive spaces that welcome people of all backgrounds while maintaining the integrity of this ancient practice.

Circle Information

Duration: 2-4 hours

Size: 8-20 participants

Setting: Comfortable, private spaces

Materials: Talking piece, centerpiece, ceremonial elements

Circle Applications

Healing from trauma, family reconciliation, workplace conflicts, school discipline issues, community planning, and celebration of achievements.

Training Available

We offer circle keeper training for community members who want to bring this practice to their organizations or neighborhoods.

Successful Transition Home

Returning to community after incarceration presents unique challenges that require comprehensive support and understanding. Our reentry programs focus on rebuilding relationships, developing practical skills, and creating sustainable support networks that help individuals successfully reintegrate into their communities.

Comprehensive Support

We provide assistance with housing, employment, education, healthcare, and legal issues while addressing the emotional and social challenges of reentry. Our approach recognizes that successful reintegration requires healing relationships with family and community members who may have been hurt by past actions.

Restorative Elements

Central to our reentry work is facilitating conversations between returning individuals and their families, victims (when appropriate), and community members. These dialogues focus on accountability, repair, and building new foundations for positive relationships moving forward.

Mentorship and Community

Each participant is matched with trained community mentors who provide ongoing support, encouragement, and practical assistance. We also connect individuals with peer support groups and community organizations that can provide long-term networks of care and accountability.

Prevention Focus

Our programs emphasize developing healthy coping strategies, conflict resolution skills, and positive community connections that reduce the likelihood of future offending. We work with participants to identify triggers and develop comprehensive safety plans.

Program Structure

Duration: 12-18 months

Intensity: Weekly meetings, as-needed support

Services: Case management, counseling, practical assistance

Cost: Free for participants

Eligibility

Adults preparing for release or recently returned to the community who are committed to positive change and building healthy relationships.

Community Partners

We collaborate with employers, housing providers, healthcare systems, and other organizations to create opportunities for successful reintegration.

Supporting Young People's Growth

Young people who cause harm often benefit more from restorative approaches than traditional punishment. Our youth programs focus on helping teenagers understand the impact of their actions, take responsibility, and develop the skills and relationships they need to make better choices in the future.

Developmentally Appropriate

We design our interventions with adolescent brain development in mind, recognizing that teenagers are still learning impulse control, consequence awareness, and empathy. Our facilitators are specially trained to work with youth and create environments that promote learning rather than shame.

Family and School Involvement

Parents, guardians, teachers, and other important adults in young people's lives are central to our process. We facilitate family conferences, school-based circles, and peer mediation programs that strengthen the support systems youth need to succeed.

Skill Building

Beyond addressing specific incidents, our programs teach conflict resolution, emotional regulation, communication skills, and leadership abilities. Youth learn to become peer mediators and restorative justice advocates in their schools and communities.

Prevention and Early Intervention

We work in schools and community centers to build restorative cultures that prevent conflicts from escalating. Our programs teach all young people skills for handling disagreements, managing emotions, and building healthy relationships.

Program Details

Ages: 12-18 years old

Settings: Schools, community centers, courts

Duration: Varies by need and program type

Approach: Strength-based, trauma-informed

Program Types

Peer mediation, youth conferences, family group conferencing, school-based circles, youth leadership development, and prevention education.

Training for Adults

We train teachers, parents, and youth workers in restorative practices so they can support young people's growth and development in all settings.

Spreading Restorative Practices

Creating lasting change requires building understanding and capacity for restorative practices throughout our community. Our education and training programs equip individuals and organizations with the knowledge and skills needed to implement restorative approaches in their daily lives and work.

Professional Training

We offer comprehensive training programs for professionals including educators, law enforcement officers, social workers, mental health professionals, and criminal justice personnel. These programs cover restorative justice principles, facilitation skills, and implementation strategies.

Community Workshops

Regular public workshops introduce community members to restorative justice concepts and practices. Topics include conflict resolution, communication skills, trauma-informed approaches, and building supportive communities. These sessions are designed to be accessible and practical.

Organizational Consultation

We work with schools, workplaces, faith communities, and other organizations to develop and implement restorative policies and practices. This includes helping organizations create systems for addressing conflicts, building community, and supporting healing when harm occurs.

Volunteer Development

Our volunteer training programs prepare community members to serve as facilitators, mentors, and advocates within our programs. We provide ongoing support and continuing education to ensure high-quality service delivery and personal growth for volunteers.

Training Options

Professional Certification: 40-hour comprehensive program

Community Workshops: 2-4 hour sessions

Organizational Training: Customized programs

Volunteer Training: 20-hour program with ongoing support

Topics Covered

Restorative justice principles, facilitation skills, trauma awareness, cultural competency, conflict transformation, and community building strategies.

Continuing Education

Monthly professional development sessions, annual conference, online resources, and peer learning networks for ongoing skill development.
