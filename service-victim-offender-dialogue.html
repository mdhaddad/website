Victim-Offender Dialogue

A path to understanding, accountability, and healing.

What It Is

Victim-Offender Dialogue (VOD) is a confidential and voluntary process that gives individuals harmed by a crime the opportunity to meet with the person who caused the harm in a safe and structured setting. Guided by a trained facilitator, this powerful dialogue allows for questions to be answered, accountability to be expressed, and a path forward to be discussed.

How It Works

The process is participant-driven and takes place only when both parties are willing and have been deemed ready by our trained staff.

  • Intake: Separate initial conversations with both the person harmed and the person who caused harm.
  • Preparation: Extensive one-on-one meetings with facilitators to prepare for the dialogue.
  • The Dialogue: A face-to-face meeting in a safe, neutral location, guided by two co-facilitators.
  • Follow-up: Ensuring any agreements are honored and providing post-dialogue support.

The Goal

The primary goal is not forgiveness, but repair. For those who were harmed, it can provide answers, a sense of empowerment, and a way to move on. For those who caused harm, it is a chance to understand the human impact of their actions, take direct responsibility, and take steps to make things right.
